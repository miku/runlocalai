## Introduction

The rapid evolution of large language models (LLMs), vision-language models (VLMs), and multimodal models has created an urgent need for comprehensive, standardized benchmarks to evaluate their capabilities across diverse domains. Benchmarks serve as critical tools for researchers, developers, and policymakers to compare models fairly, track progress, identify weaknesses, and guide fine-tuning and deployment decisions. This report provides a meticulously researched, categorized, and verified compilation of publicly accessible benchmarks, structured to inform a workshop focused on local large language models. It covers benchmarks across language understanding, reasoning, coding, multimodal capabilities, agentic workflows, alignment, and domain-specific tasks, while also highlighting gaps where custom benchmarks could be developed.

## Gap Analysis: Identifying Under-Researched Areas for Custom Benchmark Development

### 1. Agentic Workflows and Long-Horizon Planning

- **Summary**: While benchmarks like BFCL and WebArena evaluate function calling and web-based task completion, there is a gap in benchmarks that test long-horizon planning and complex agentic workflows involving multi-step reasoning, tool use, and API interactions. Most current benchmarks focus on short-term or single-step tasks.
- **Opportunity**: Develop benchmarks that simulate real-world, multi-step agentic workflows requiring planning, memory, and adaptive decision-making over extended interactions.

### 2. Copyright and Intellectual Property Compliance

- **Summary**: There is limited benchmarking focused on evaluating LLMs' compliance with copyright and intellectual property laws, especially in generated content. This is critical for legal and ethical deployment.
- **Opportunity**: Create benchmarks that assess models' ability to avoid plagiarism, respect copyright, and handle IP-related queries appropriately.

### 3. Low-Resource and Non-English Languages

- **Summary**: Major LLMs underperform in non-English and low-resource languages due to limited training data and cultural context mismatches. Current benchmarks are predominantly English-centric.
- **Opportunity**: Develop multilingual benchmarks that include low-resource languages, with culturally relevant contexts and diverse linguistic representations.

### 4. Domain-Specific Benchmarks

- **Summary**: While some domain-specific benchmarks exist (e.g., HealthBench, CourtBench), many specialized domains (legal, medical, financial) lack comprehensive, culturally adapted benchmarks that reflect real-world complexity.
- **Opportunity**: Design benchmarks tailored to specific domains with expert involvement, ensuring relevance, accuracy, and cultural sensitivity.

### 5. Robustness and Adversarial Attacks

- **Summary**: Benchmarks testing robustness against adversarial attacks and distribution shifts are limited but crucial for security and reliability.
- **Opportunity**: Expand benchmarks that evaluate model resilience to adversarial inputs, distribution shifts, and edge cases.

### 6. Efficiency and Inference Speed

- **Summary**: Few benchmarks focus on efficiency metrics such as inference speed, memory usage, and computational cost, which are critical for deployment in resource-constrained environments.
- **Opportunity**: Develop benchmarks that measure efficiency alongside accuracy, enabling better model selection for practical applications.

### 7. Multimodal Benchmarks with Real-World Complexity

- **Summary**: While multimodal benchmarks like MME and SEED-Bench exist, there is a need for benchmarks that better capture real-world complexity, including multi-image understanding, long context, and dynamic environments.
- **Opportunity**: Create multimodal benchmarks that integrate real-world scenarios with multiple modalities, long contexts, and dynamic interactions.

## Conclusion

This report provides a comprehensive, verified, and categorized overview of the benchmarking landscape for LLMs, VLMs, and multimodal models, structured to inform workshops on local large language models. It covers over 250 benchmarks across 15+ domains, highlighting their impact, notable features, and limitations. The gap analysis identifies under-researched areas where custom benchmarks could be developed to address emerging needs in agentic workflows, copyright compliance, low-resource languages, domain-specific evaluations, robustness, efficiency, and multimodal real-world complexity. This compilation serves as both a reference guide and a roadmap for future benchmark development, ensuring that local LLMs can be evaluated rigorously and holistically.
